# Getting Started with Wikipefia

Wikipefia is your academic companion — a knowledge base of articles, guides, and resources organized by subject and teacher. Here's how to make the most of it.

## How to Navigate

<Tabs>
<Tab label="By Subject">
Click on any subject name to see its front page with an overview, article categories, and teacher information. Each subject organizes articles into categories like "Foundations" and "Advanced Topics."
</Tab>

<Tab label="By Teacher">
Click on a teacher's name to see their profile, ratings, reviews, contact information, and any articles they've written (study guides, exam tips, etc.).
</Tab>

<Tab label="By Search">
Press **Cmd+K** (Mac) or **Ctrl+K** (Windows/Linux) to open the search dialog. Search across all subjects, teachers, and articles instantly. Results are grouped by type and show relevance.
</Tab>
</Tabs>

## Quick Search Tips

<Callout type="info">
The search indexes all article titles, descriptions, and keywords. Try searching for topics like "eigenvalues", "sorting algorithms", or a teacher's name.
</Callout>

- Search is **instant** — results appear as you type
- Results are grouped: Subjects, Teachers, Articles
- Search works in your current language
- Press **Enter** to go to the first result, or use arrow keys to navigate

## Language Settings

Wikipefia supports three languages: **Russian** (default), **English**, and **Czech**. Use the language switcher in the top navigation bar to change.

<Callout type="warning">
Not all articles are available in all languages. When an article isn't available in your chosen language, you'll see a banner showing which language the content is displayed in.
</Callout>

## Content Types

| Type | What It Is | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Subject** | A university course with organized articles | Linear Algebra |
| **Teacher** | A professor's profile with ratings and articles | Ivan Petrov |
| **System Article** | A standalone guide or reference | This page! |

## Keyboard Shortcuts

| Shortcut | Action |
|---|---|
| `Cmd/Ctrl + K` | Open search |
| `Escape` | Close search / dialog |
| `↑ / ↓` | Navigate search results |
| `Enter` | Go to selected result |
